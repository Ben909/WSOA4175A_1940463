Provincial Covid-19 Recoveries

As per the assignment brief, we are required to produce some form of data visualization using an existing API. The graphs below is facilitated by the open source library, D3.js to illustrate the statistical distribution of recovered Covid-19 patients across all nine provinces of South-Africa. The concept for the chart is inspired by the times we life in. Amidst the pandemic, the current change in climate proves that more patients have recovered due to a lower chance for the virus to spread in warm weather temperatures. I therefore decided to create a chart which indicates which provinces had the highest number of total recoveries. My idea was that this information could be used inform communities of strategies to combat the virus or mitigate risk factors.For the purpose of this visualization, there are no databases where I can store this information or keep it updated. Further development of this chart must be conducted within these considerations.

Circle Diagram

For my circle diagram, I incorporated the same dataset as in the previous bar chart to create a new visualization. My goal with the development of this chart is to express how alternative methods can be used to visualize datasets. This gives an alternative perspective to the end-user about the information they are presented.Although not fully operational, my experimentation with new visualizations revealed the importance of data science. If we are to break down most visualizations, we find that most are made up of shapes, colors and sizable dimensions. It is important to note how influential these elements are to make informative decisions and how this impacts the visualization of information. Coherence with regard to the building blocks of visualizations gives it the ability to convey a story more efficiently.

Stacked disease Chart

Stacked charts are useful for displaying information that can be compared as a whole. These graphs use multiple groups of data to identify the patterns between them. The representation of these categories also helps to situate each group and its relevance within the overarching narrative of the graph. The bottom range of this chart represents age groups and the number of underlying medical conditions are displayed on the Y-Axis. The goal of this graph is meant to represent the number people who had previous conditions before contracting the Covid-19 virus. It gives more awareness to the importance of social distancing through the fact that mortality rates of some groups are significantly higher based on their medical background.

Alternative Bar Chart comparison

From personal experience with visualizations, bar charts are easy to interpret since the relationship between bar sizes and their corresponding values enable users to easily identify and mark comparisons. For this reason, I created a simple bar chart showcasing the number of Covid-19 tests performed by the top ten most infected counties. The statistical values and the relationship between these countries help to establish stories about how we combat the virus. With their population taken into account, it is natural that countries with higher inflections will perform more tests. Herein lies some of the disadvantages of bar charts. They tend to look at particular data sets, which makes it hard to represent wide ranges of numbers to efficiently communicate their message. The population of India (1,385,419,430) is more than four times the population of the USA (331,780,397). Yet the number of tests performed in India is drastically lower in comparison to other populations. It sets a narrative to other information presented about Covid-19.
