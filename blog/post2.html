Data Visualization Histories

In the earliest forms of data visualizations, scientists meant to record and measure living representations that offer statistical value on how we view information. There is much debate on what is meant by the definition of data visualization. We typically understand it as different ways of representing some form of collected data that helps us to make decisions or navigate in the real world (Friendly: 2006). From the history of data visualizations, it becomes clear that these representations develop how we communicate knowledge to provide a graphical overview of interrelated concepts.

Geometric diagrams were one of the first data visualizations used to formulate maps and navigational structures for the purpose of exploration. The expeditioners would use celestial coordinates to plot and depict locations as a standard form of reference for their surveys. Later forms of geographical visualizations present a spherical view of the earth, where scientists measure longitude through arcs or degrees to visualize day- and nighttime periods at various locations. These techniques of measurement and observation in the Middle ages are evident in a number of topographical and statistical tables used by scholars throughout the millennia. This expansion of analytic geometry and estimation broadened how scientists were able to visually represent empirical data. The continued distribution of these visualizations resulted in new methods and theories of how statistical data representations are be collected.

The abstract development of graphs, along with technological innovations enabled the reproduction and dissemination of graphic works by the year of 1794. (Friendly: 2006) At this stage, the accumulation of data forms progressed toward the development of modern information sources such as atlases and comprehensive maps used to represent quantitative datasets. This would ultimately open the door for new areas of examination as complex analysis of datasets gave rise to further developments on how we apply different forms of data visualization. With refined administration, the representative value of graphic forms became more justified as comparisons between datasets opened new dimensions for observation. By the years 1950 to 1975, research collaborations offered the means to produce widespread interactive applications which featured command driven and statistical approaches to the collection of data sources (Friendly: 2006).
